Artificial Intelligence A In 1997, the world chess champion, Garry Kasparov, was defeated in a six game match by a computer program known as Deep Blue, despite having played against computers before. People feared that this the beginning of a world where the machines we created could now out-think us. B For decades, artificial intelligence (AI) convenience to science fiction, which featured human- like computers with silicon brains. In addition, it was the subject of deep debate about what makes us human: personality, emotions, intelligence and free will. However, modern artificial intelligence is more about engineering than philosophy, and is concerned with finding practical solutions to everyday problems. These days artificial intelligence involves writing computer programs that make computers and robots more useful in ways we term 'intelligent'. In fact, a computer may be able to beat a chess champion, but it would still fail in a chat with a five year old child. C Today, a variety of designers, engineers and planners use artificial intelligence in many ways. Artificial intelligence programs are also used for things such as diagnosing medical conditions, creating travel itineraries, predicting share prices and planning resource use in business. Artificial intelligence programs schedule things such as public transport and school timetables.
